Who is LABBRA?

Six lives, one mission - Click any letter to learn their story

L

London

A bright light taken too soon

London was a vibrant student whose life was tragically cut short in a pedestrian accident. Their memory serves as a powerful reminder of why pedestrian safety technology is so crucial.

"London's spirit lives on in every life we save through better technology and awareness."

The London Foundation continues their legacy by advocating for safer streets and supporting families affected by pedestrian accidents. Their work focuses on education, awareness, and pushing for better infrastructure to protect pedestrians.

London's story represents the 'L' in LABBRA - standing for Life-saving technology that detects pedestrian accidents in real-time, ensuring immediate emergency response.

A

Alexandria

Remembered for their kindness and determination

Alexandria was known for their compassionate nature and dedication to helping others. Their tragic loss in a pedestrian accident has motivated our team to develop technology that could prevent similar tragedies.

"Alexandria's memory drives us to create a world where no family has to experience this loss."

In honor of Alexandria, the 'A' in LABBRA represents Advanced AI-powered detection systems that use machine learning to identify accidents with precision and speed.

Their legacy continues through our commitment to developing the most sophisticated pedestrian safety technology possible, ensuring that every crosswalk becomes a safer place for everyone.

B

Brooke

A memorial that reminds us daily

Brooke Dawkins' memorial stands as a daily reminder of the pedestrian safety crisis near UCF campus. Their story has become a rallying point for change and safer infrastructure.

"Every time we pass Brooke's memorial, we're reminded why this work matters."

The Brooke Dawkins Foundation works tirelessly to improve pedestrian safety through advocacy, education, and supporting technological solutions like LABBRA Systems.

Brooke's name represents the 'B' in LABBRA - standing for Breakthrough technology that bridges the gap between accident occurrence and emergency response, potentially saving countless lives.

Visit Brooke Dawkins Foundation →
B

Brandon

A guardian spirit watching over our streets

Brandon's impact on the UCF community was profound. Their loss in a pedestrian accident has inspired a movement toward better safety measures and technological solutions.

"Brandon's spirit watches over every intersection we protect."

This second 'B' in LABBRA represents Beacon systems that serve as watchful guardians on our streets, monitoring 24/7 for pedestrian safety and ready to respond at a moment's notice.

Brandon's memory motivates our team to ensure that our technology serves as a protective beacon, just as Brandon was a beacon of light in their community.

R

Reuel

Speed and response in their honor

Reuel's story emphasizes the critical importance of rapid response in emergency situations. Their loss has taught us that every second counts when it comes to saving lives.

"In Reuel's memory, we work to make every second count."

The 'R' in LABBRA stands for Rapid response capabilities that reduce emergency service arrival times by up to 40%, potentially saving countless lives through faster intervention.

Reuel's legacy lives on in our commitment to developing the fastest, most reliable emergency response system possible, ensuring that help arrives when it's needed most.

A

Austin

Spreading awareness and hope

Austin completes our LABBRA family, representing the final piece of our mission. Their story reminds us that awareness and education are just as important as technology.

"Austin's legacy is one of awareness, education, and hope for a safer future."

This final 'A' in LABBRA represents Awareness - spreading knowledge about pedestrian safety and the technology that can protect our communities.

Austin's memory drives our outreach efforts, ensuring that communities understand both the risks and the solutions available to protect pedestrians at every crosswalk.
